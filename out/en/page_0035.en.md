## English (Meaning Translation)

...nor were there any good means of transport.

To fulfill the shortage of Sikh preaching from many places, appeals used to come to Chief Khalsa Diwan, on which the Diwan used to send Bhai Hira Singh Ji to such places.
In **March 1903**, on the appeal of local Sangats of **Peshawar**, efforts made for that...
**Appeal:** Beloved Panth! There is a dire need of preaching in the area of Peshawar. Especially in Peshawar (which is a center of Sikhism), Sikhism is declining day by day... sacrificing sacred hair they are entering the [Arya] Samaj. Seeing many such miseries, it has been considered that all these deficits are due to lack of preaching...

Reaching places like **Mardan**, **Garhi Kapura**, **Rustam**, **Akora** etc., [he] did Gurmat preaching for one month.
[Opponents] created a lot of noise and tried to disrupt the Diwan, but when Bhai Hira Singh Ji stood up and answered tactfully, everyone became quiet. In this side, there was much hatred spread against Singh Sabha, but with the preaching of this Jatha, people embraced it.

Similarly, in 'Khalsa Samachar' dated 24 April 1904, the following report is found under the headline **'Preaching in Kahuta'** from **Sardar Asa Singh Ji**:
In this town, for the last five days **Bhai Bhag Singh Ji, Bhai Hira Singh Ji** have come, who through their captivating Kirtan have waxed the stone hearts of the Sangat to such an extent that... who was that heart which listening to...
Listening to the love-filled Kirtan... dispassion [arose]...
