## English (Meaning Translation)

(Khalsa Samachar, 29-10-19[19?]).

**Participation in Panthic Service**: Soon after the birth of Bhai Hira Singh Ji, **Bhai Bhag Singh Ji** started his Kirtan tours in the area of **Pothohar**. In those days [Sants?] were preaching a lot in the areas. Bhai Bhag Singh Ji contributed according to his capacity.

In the year **1897**, Bhai Bhag Singh Ji started performing Kirtan joining with his son **Bhai Hira Singh Ji**, and then for 25-26 years, reaching countries within the nation and abroad, they sounded the drum of Guru Nanak's Name and connected countless souls to the Guru's feet.

Their sweet melodious throat, high vibrating tune and the Gurbani sung in simple tunes of proper Raag used to bring a wave in the congregations. When in the solitude of Amrit Vela (early morning), **Bapu Ji** (Bhai Bhag Singh Ji was often remembered by the name 'Bapu Ji') used to move the bow on the **Taus** and sing the alap in a sweet voice, then the hearts of the listeners used to get pulled.

Bhai Bhag Singh Ji was a master of Raag. There was a special distinct juice (Ras) in his throat; he was very firm in rhythm and beat (Sur-Taal) and used to get engrossed in Kirtan while performing.
According to the late **Master Karam Singh Ji**, Assistant Secretary Chief Khalsa Diwan, his father was instrumental in filling the power of Kirtan in the consciousness of Bhai Hira Singh Ji, and with the blessing of Kirtan, taking Bhai Hira Singh Ji to heights...

Once at Sri Akal Takht, [a competition?] between some famous Ragis and Bhai Bhag Singh Ji... [The condition was] to sing in the same Raag as written in Sri Guru Granth Sahib. Many... were sung, but in the end Bhai Bhag Singh [succeeded] in this...
