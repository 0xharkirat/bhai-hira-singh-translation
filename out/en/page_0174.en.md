**Some Excerpts from Articles Published on Birth Centenary and After**
**[Bhai Hira] Singh Ji Ragi**
*Giani Gurdit Singh, Singh Sabha Patrika, November 1979*

Bhai Hira Singh were a Ragi and also a Vairagi Yogi, the tune of whose Kingri (instrument/voice) rang in the hearts of infinite Sikhs. His Kirtan made many 'Vairagi' (detached/devotional) and connected them with Guru's House. 'Ragi' means one who knows the knowledge of Rag; the second meaning of 'Rag' is 'Love'. The person who becomes detached from worldly love is called 'Vairagi'. Bhai Hira Singh was a Ragi of Love. A 'Vairagi' who detaches the seekers from the world, and a 'Yogi' who connects them with Gurbani and the Source of Relish. Due to these virtues, he had captivated the era. ...

**The Great Kirtaniya of Guru: Bhai Hira Singh Ji Ragi**
*S. Amir Singh Nur, M.A., Sri Ganganagar*
*'Khalsa Samachar', 28 August 1980*

...A small settlement Chitraur, which was about three miles from Firuka. All of us students of the school [went] along with Bhai Sahib's Kirtani Jatha...

## Notes
- 'Kingri': String instrument associated with Yogis; here used metaphorically for his voice/Kirtan.
- Giani Gurdit Singh analyzes the wordplay: Ragi (Musician/Lover) vs Vairagi (Detached) vs Yogi (United).
