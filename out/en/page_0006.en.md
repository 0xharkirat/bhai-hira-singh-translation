**Preface**
(First Edition)

Many such humble and virtuous men have passed through the Sikh community who preached the religion, whose life achievements could create glorious traditions for the community, and whose biographies could serve as a lighthouse for future generations. But...

Many Gursikhs sacrificed their all—wealth, virtues, and personal health—to awaken the sleeping community after the fall of the Sikh Raj. If the accounts of their services do not come into written form today, then after another half-century, their memory or even their names and addresses will be lost forever.

Among such God-oriented Gursikhs and devoted servants, one name is that of the humble servant of the Panth, 'Bhai Sahib Bhai Hira Singh Ji Ragi', who performed tireless service for the growth of the Panth in every aspect—religious, social, and educational—in his time.

Many efforts were made to write a complete biography of Bhai Sahib, but they could not reach completion. First of all, after his departure for the Guru's Abode, in September 1926, Giani Hira Singh Dard (1889-1965), the editor of the monthly journal 'Phulwari', asked the author of these lines (who was then only a First Year student at Khalsa College) about some circumstances regarding Bhai Sahib. He published an article in the October 1926 issue under the title 'Crown Jewel of Sikh Preachers' along with a photo of him, and asked me to research and bring detailed news. Along with this, he also gave an indication at the end of his article about publishing a full biography in this hope. But this could not happen.

In the year 1931-32, Sriman Bhai Sahib Bhai Vir Singh Ji ordered me: "If you can find any diaries or handwritten notes of Bhai Hira Singh Ji, bring them to us." When I went to my village Faruka during the next summer vacations, I searched every corner of the house and found a daily journal written in his own hand, which was in the form of a register.
