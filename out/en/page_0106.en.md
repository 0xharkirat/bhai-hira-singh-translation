## English (Meaning Translation)

**Mata Bishan Kaur Ji (Karachi/Sindh):**
When Bhai Hira Singh Ji used to go for preaching in Sindh... Gurdwara...
According to custom there, this widow lady was forbidden to go out of home. So sitting in her home only she used to enjoy the Kirtan! This [Kirtan]... her...
Then she started reading Gur-History and tracts of **Khalsa Tract Society**. For many years this lady continued walking on path of Sikhism while being at home. Finally she could not resist and once when Bhai Hira Singh Ji went to that town again, she convinced her parents and went to Gurdwara to attend Kirtan. Slowly the color [of Naam] kept rising and on one occasion of Amrit Parchar, she took Amrit and her name was changed from **Bishan Dei** to **Bishan Kaur**. After some time her father also took Amrit and became **Thahriya Singh** from **Seth Thahriya Mal**.

This lady then started preaching Sikhism in Sindh, later established a Preaching Center in **Karachi** and got a Gurdwara built in neighborhood (Mohalla) **Kharadar**...
She used to lecture in [Sindhi?] words such that no one could say she was not Sindhi.
Attending **Sikh Educational Conference** congregations, she used to [promote] female education and...
Strong emotions for love of Sikhism used to rise in her.
(Adopted a child named **Keshdhari Singh**). Spirit of Sikhism was filled in this child so much...

## Notes
- Story of **Mata Bishan Kaur**, a Sindhi widow who was inspired by Bhai Hira Singh's Kirtan (initially listening from home due to purdah/custom). She became a prominent preacher and established a Gurdwara in Karachi.
