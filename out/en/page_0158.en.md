...His name will always be counted in the Panth [among those] who had the glory of doing maximum work in the religious and educational progress of the Panth...
...prayer is that Waheguru may grant residence to your soul in His feet...

*Akali Pardesi, September 1926*

...Bhai Hira Singh Ji's tongue used to work like magic, and not only every Sikh but even fanatic Hindu brothers, hearing his speech, would cry out "Ash-Ash" (Praise be!).
...No part [of the country] was visible where Bhai Sahib had not reached...
...and not sounded the Naad (conch/voice) of Guru Nanak...

*Khalsa Samachar, 9.6.1927*

When the Uncrowned King of the Panth, Sardar Kharak Singh Ji, was released from jail on June 8, 1927, his grand procession was taken out. On the morning of June 9, at Jallianwala Bagh...
...Speaking about his sacrifice in the address of the [SGPC?] Committee, Bhai Sahib...
Sardar Kharak Singh Ji said in his reply... "Those who have gone after giving a show/glimpse... like Bhai Hira Singh... Sikhs can never forget them..."

## Notes
- "Ash-Ash": An exclamation of wonder/praise (Wah-Wah).
- Mentions S. Kharak Singh's tribute upon his release from jail.
