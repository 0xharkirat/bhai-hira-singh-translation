## English (Meaning Translation)

**Haji Muhammad Maskin and Sandalwood Whisk (Chaur):**
**Sir Mahadani Sardar Bahadur Dharam Singh Ji (1870-1933?)** ...was a Nitnemi Gursikh. He had immense love for **Sri Sant Attar Singh** and **Bhai Hira Singh**, and was a lover of their Kirtan.
In the second decade of 20th century when [work] of making **Delhi** the capital started... he started work. The red stone used on Government buildings of New Delhi, **Parliament House**, **Secretariat** and **Rashtrapati Bhavan** etc. is all done by Sir Dharam Singh Ji. Earning good profit in business, he spent immense wealth on religious works. Forming **Guru Nanak Vidya Bhandar Trust**, he started Gurmat Vidyala and many schools which are running beautifully till now. He built beautiful buildings of almost all historical Gurdwaras of **Delhi**.

In **December 1925**, on invitation of **Sardar Dharam Singh Ji Contractor**, Bhai Hira Singh Ji had gone to **Bharatpur** and from there Gwalior Sangat took him for Diwan program. There a Muslim Fakir, **Haji Muhammad Maskin** was staying.
Though Sai essentially belonged to **Lahore** but for more than half [century?] he lived in **Mecca** and **Medina**... and prepared a **Sandalwood Chaur (Whisk)** with his own hands there...
...after departure to Gurpuri... on **23.9.1926**, described like this:
"Hardly a year would have passed that an elderly Muslim Fakir, whose age was about **70 years**..."

## Notes
- "S. Dharam Singh Contractor" - Famous philanthropist and builder of New Delhi.
- "Haji Muhammad Maskin" - A Muslim Fakir who made a unique Sandalwood Chaur for Golden Temple.
