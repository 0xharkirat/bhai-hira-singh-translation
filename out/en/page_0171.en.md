*'Khalsa Samachar' 6.9.1973*

...It is a matter of about 56-57 years ago that a Diwan was decorated in the hall of Khalsa High School Faruka. A Hindu brother stood up and raised an objection that "You Sikhs have started refuting the rituals of Hindu Dharma from the opposite side." Then he recited these lines from Gurbani:
*"Raj Gopal Pandit Sadyeh Har Har Katha Parda Puran Jio..."* (Call the King, the Gopal, the Pandit, read the Har Har Katha and Puranas...)
*"Pind Pattal Kria Deeva Phul Har Sar Pavaye..."* (Rice balls, leafy plates, rites, lamps, flowers - offer them to the pool of God...)
*(Guru Granth Sahib Ang 923)*

After this, that gentleman sat down, but Bhai Sahib stood up on the stage. You [recited] the lines accompanying the above lines, which that brother had left out:
*(Guru Granth Sahib Ang 358)* [Note: The text cites 358 but the previous quote is from 923. The rebuttal likely clarified the context of 'Har Sar' vs empty rituals].

And quoted many proofs from history. How impressive your explanation was, only those gentlemen know who have heard it. The magic of this...
...The Hindu brother who was criticizing became a devotee of the Guru's House.
...With folded hands, he asked for the gift of Amrit. The whole congregation was stunned.
...I cannot say with certainty what the name of that brother was, but... Sial...

**Bibi Harnam Kaur Ji**
