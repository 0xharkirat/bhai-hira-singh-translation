## English (Meaning Translation)

**Backdrop of Singh Sabha Movement**
With the end of Sikh Raj, one [factor was that] Sikhs themselves had fallen into decline.
Attacks of non-believers and missionaries... like termite were eating [the Panth]. The number of Sikhs had fallen to such a low that where during Sikh Raj the count was near one crore, in 1881 it remained only 1,853,437.

On one side the preaching of Christian missionaries and on the other contrary practices...
In the Parikarma of Sri Darbar Sahib, idols were worshipped...
In 1873... **Pandit Shardha Ram Phillauri** (1839-1881), the author of Hindu Aarti 'Om Jai Jagdish Hare', while performing Katha in Guru Ka Bagh, spoke some words against the glory of Guru Sahibs. This was like the last straw on the camel's back. The blood of Khalsa boiled. As a result,
To discuss these issues, **Sardar Thakur Singh Sandhawalia** along with other prominent Sikhs called a meeting...
...Four Sikh students were taken to England and converted to Christianity...
After staying there for about one and a half years, re-converting them to Sikh religion...
Later in May 19XX... Maharaja [Duleep Singh?]...

A big Diwan was held, in which prominent gentlemen of the Panth participated and **Sri Guru Singh Sabha** was established. This was the beginning of Singh Sabha Lahore.
Then in 1879 Singh Sabha Lahore was formed and Singh Sabhas were established at places outside also. In 1903 a representative Jatha of Sabhas [formed] Khalsa Diwan...
