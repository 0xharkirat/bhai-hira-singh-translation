## English (Meaning Translation)

He stayed for about one and a half years in the feet of Saints [Sant Attar Singh Ji?]. He went to Sindh with the Sant Ji...
After spending some months in the company of the Saint... and used to be engrossed in Simran... Bhai Hira Singh Ji also used to participate in Bhajan...

Then when the Jatha of Saints [returned]... appeared in the Dera... and requested that Bhai Hira Singh is a "Hira" (Diamond), give blessing that staying at his own home he may recite Naam and make others recite. ...Saints gave blessings to Bhai Hira Singh along with Bapu Ji and sent them off.

**Return to Faruka:**
When he reached his village Faruka, the residents of the town were very pleased to see his saintly attire and saintly nature. A white cotton tunic (Gatra) on the body, Kachhera up to knees, a round white turban on head and Gatra-Kirpan in neck, an iron Gadva (pot) in one hand and Simrana (rosary) in the other - all these were very impressive.
When... people gathered and said, "The [son] of Bhai Bhag Singh... amazes everyone." Mast eyes and radiant [face]...
Bhai Sahib's mother Sat Bharai used to say... "Whatever words come out of the mouth of this 'Sadhu', they come true." ...
Mata Ji used to say... "Attire and signs are like saints... If he stays in this field (household)..."

**Departure for Sri Amritsar:**
Finally, how long could this son of a Saint stay in his home? The atmosphere he had left behind in the company of Sant Attar Singh Ji, where could he find it [here]? Dispassion kept arising in the inner self and mind did not settle in Faruka. The passion for Satsang kept getting stronger in the heart. Convincing his wife to get ready to go to **Sri Amritsar**, one day he asked for permission from his parents. Father tried hard, Mother pleaded a lot...
