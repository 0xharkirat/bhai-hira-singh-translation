## English (Meaning Translation)
...[Those who] invest their body, mind, and wealth for the fulfillment of this motive...
...Among these was one, the shining star of the Sikh Panth, the late Bhai Sahib Bhai Hira Singh Ji Ragi, whose light of Panthic service will always keep giving illumination.

*'Khalsa Samachar', 30.8.1951*

...
...His soul [may be granted residence in] the Feet [of the Master]...
...

**One Selfless and Desireless [Servant]**
*Rai Bahadur Sardar Bisakha Singh Ji Delhi,*
*'Sher-E-Punjab' 2 September 1951 [1921?]*

...In the year 1898, I heard Bhai Hira Singh Ji's Kirtan for the first time, when he, in his youth, had gone to village Chhajjalwaddi from Amritsar for a wedding.
...The Kirtan he did... all the listeners were extremely impressed...
...When he used to do Kirtan on the new moon night (Masya), people would [gather in large numbers?]...

## Notes
- Rai Bahadur Bisakha Singh recalls his first meeting in 1898. This gives a timeline of Bhai Hira Singh's early career.
- Mention of "Nishkam" (Selfless).
