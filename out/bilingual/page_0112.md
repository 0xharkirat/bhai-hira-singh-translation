## English (Meaning Translation)

'...so many Sangat came to listen that in such huge courtyard of Gurdwara there was no place left to sit.
**Master Karam Singh Ji**... states...
In **Peshawar**... opponents threw stones... Bhai Sahib uttered with love-soaked, humble and sweet throat, 'Khalsa Ji! Remain calm, people throw stones at berry tree, it gives berries; and if someone strikes a Sikh with stone, why should something not be received from there?' Coming in emotion he said, 'I am a humble Nanak-Leva Sikh... My prayer to Guru Nanak Dev Ji is that He may bless these stone-throwers with seven [notes?] from Mardana's Rabab...'
...Stone throwers felt ashamed...
Next day... **Bhai Dittu Singh**... sat in upper gallery... threw handfuls of rupees three-four times... 'Sacrificed on these feet...'

**S. Harnam Singh, Nayi Basti Kasur** writes in Khalsa Samachar of 30.8.1951:
[A man] took a vow that from today I will absolutely not [consume] tobacco...
When friends said that this tobacco smoking instrument (Hookah)...
He replied that now I will not even touch it. Then it was seen that... he never smoked tobacco, after some time he became a Sikh...
It was wonder that such effect on a foolish man...

**Qureshi Ali Mardan Shah Advocate:**
In welcome address of **Sikh Educational Conference (Rawalpindi/Gujranwala?)** now Pakistan, March **1929**...
...influence of speech...

## Notes
- Repetition of the Peshawar stone-throwing incident and Bhai Dittu Singh's offering.
- **S. Harnam Singh** of Kasur narrates the story of a tobacco smoker giving up the habit and becoming a Sikh.
- Introduction of **Qureshi Ali Mardan Shah Advocate** praising Bhai Hira Singh in 1929.
