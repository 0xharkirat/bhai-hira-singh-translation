## English (Meaning Translation)

...On that day such a large number of poor and rich Sehajdhari Sikhs taking Amrit and [becoming Singhs], this was not the [magic] of Sant Attar Singh Ji and Bhai Hira Singh Ji alone.
...It was an effort made with the support of [Guru's Feet], which was a miracle.

Bhai Hira Singh Ji usually used to sing a Ghazal in memory of Sahib Kalgidhar Ji with great faith and devotion. It is this:

*Mai Lekar Teg-e-Barahna, Kalgidhar Ji Aate Hain*
(Taking a naked sword, Kalgidhar Ji comes)
...
*Pakad Khench Kar Satgur Us Ko Tambu Mein Le Jate Hain*
(Catching and pulling, Satguru takes him into the tent)
*Algarz Sabh Sangat Mein Se, Sikh Guru Ke Nikle Paanch!*
(In short, from the whole congregation, five Sikhs of the Guru came out!)
*Un Ko Daste Mubarak Se Phir Aake Hayat Pilate Hain*
(Then coming back, he feeds them Life [Amrit] from his blessed hands)
*Abdi Jeevan Un Ko Bakhsha, Aaj Talak Woh Zinda Hain*
(He bestowed eternal life upon them, they are alive till today)
*Un Ke Sadke Hamre Jaise Papi Bhi Gat Pate Hain*
(By their grace, even sinners like us get salvation)

Actually, there was a passion and a yearning inside Bhai Hira Singh Ji that the preaching of Sikhism should happen in the whole world. In this regard, some words of one of his speeches are as follows, which were given in the Golden Jubilee Book of Sikh Educational Conference:
Appealing [he] said, "Khalsa will roar like a lion in the forest and bushes... When will these great words come true?
In the world, the wealthy and powerful are in unrest, those who take pride in cannons and guns are seeing themselves empty inside. This heat of the world has to be removed, and peace in the world has to come through your hands and your Sikh religion. Arise, get organized and jump into Dharma Prachar and show by becoming at least one crore Khalsa.
The work of Dharma Prachar should not be left sitting in hope of Granthis of Gurdwaras and preachers countable on fingers, this work should be done by every Sardar, Raja, Maharaja, worker and businessman."
