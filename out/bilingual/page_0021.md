## English (Meaning Translation)

**Bhai Bhag Singh Ji** (1844-1947) was the only son of his parents.
Younger to him was his sister **Piar Bai**. During the childhood of child Bhag Singh, his father had to stay away for many months in connection with his business, due to which the upbringing of his childhood days largely came under the share of his mother. Mata Ji was a great lover of Naam Bani. In the Dharamsala of Bhai Jeet Singh Ji, Mata Ji got child Bhag Singh educated in Gurmukhi-Gurbani. Being the only son of parents, he was very pampered in the house. Father and grandfather were among the famous gentlemen of their area, due to which the early life of Bhag Singh was spent in comfort and he had the liberty to do as he pleased. His inclination was towards Raag (music), his throat was melodious and he was fond of singing and playing music. So, when Baba Jiwan Singh Ji went for his business, Bhag Singh used to spend the day in the company of the village singers.

Behind their house was a shrine (Mazar) of a Muslim Fakir, which was famous by the name of **Mahabshah**. Every year a big fair used to be held here and Muslims from far and wide [used to come]. [One musician name lost]... whose singing and playing was much liked by the area and he was kept here only.
Bhag Singh Ji started living in his company. Spending whole days learning proper Raag tunes from that Mirasi and also learned to play Sarangi and then Sitar. In some time, he acquired mastery in Raag. Baba Jiwan Singh tried hard that Bhag Singh Ji should take interest in his business, but he did not adopt the father's profession of shopkeeping. He remained engrossed in his singing and playing. Finally Baba Jiwan Singh Ji persuaded him that if you have to do singing and playing, then sing only the Baani of Guru Maharaj which is all in Raags, and perform Kirtan of Gurbani.

In the Dharamsala of [Bhai] Jeet Singh Ji which was near the house in the same street, [he started performing] Asa Ki Var... He started playing **Taus** and then used this instrument all his life. Thus, molding his art and skill into the Kirtan of Gurbani, he spent his long life in the service of the Panth.

Bhai Bhag Singh Ji was married around the year 1860 at a young age to **Sat Bharai Ji**, the elder daughter of Bhai Bhag Singh Ji Gand of Faruka. For a long time, no child was born in their house and the first child **Sri Sant Bhai Ram Kishan Ji**...
