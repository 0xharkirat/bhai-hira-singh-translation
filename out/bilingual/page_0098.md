## English (Meaning Translation)

**Sri Sant Attar Singh Ji Mastuana Wale (1866-1927)** was a great soul of meditation and earnings of Naam. Light used to drip from his face. His first visit to **Faruka** is written to be in **August 1903**, when he stayed in Faruka for full 18 days and showered rain of Kirtan and also accomplished a difficult Anand Karaj.

During these very days, one evening, Sri Sant Ji while walking went and sat on the top of the 'Pakka Khu' (bricked well) and got absorbed in Simran. When Samadhi opened, he made a utterance (Bachan), "Here some Gurmukh has practiced meditation, the Chit (consciousness) has blossomed."
Bhai Hira Singh Ji kept this thing in his heart and later buying that piece of land, got a **Gufa** (cave/meditation cell) built at that place.
The service of this Gufa, Bhai Sahib performed along with Sangat of the area while reciting Gurbani.
This Gufa was a small two-storied building, under which there was a basement (tekhana) and above a room (Chaubara). After that whenever Sri Sant Ji used to come to Faruka, [he] used to sit in this Gufa's solitary place to do Kirtan and Bhajan Simran and enjoy spiritual bliss. Sometimes some Satsangis also joined.

**Last Meeting:**
In **July-August 1926**, when Bhai Hira Singh Ji [was in] **Delhi**...

- Relation with Sant Attar Singh Ji Mastuana.
- Construction of a 'Gufa' in Faruka at a spot identified by Sant Ji as spiritually charged.
