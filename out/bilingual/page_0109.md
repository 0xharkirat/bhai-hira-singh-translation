## English (Meaning Translation)

*Birtha Kaho Kaun Sio Man Ki || Ardas...*
(To whom should I tell the condition of my mind... Ardas...)
(Guru Granth Sahib, Ang 810)

In happiness [of winning case], Bhai Hira Singh Ji reached **Faruka** and taking all children of school to that disputed land held Diwans. Hoisting **Khalsa Flag**, the name of this land was kept **Panth Garh**. Bhai Amar Singh Ji fed Langar to children with his own hands. This incident is of **1923** and great part of this... was written and sent by **Sardar Sucha Singh Sandhu M.A.** of **Jalandhar**, an old student of Faruka school. This gentleman was present in those Diwans and Ardas.

At the final time of **Bhai Amar Singh Ji**, according to his wish, continuous recitation of Gurbani, Bhajan Simran and Kirtan flow continued near him for **13 days**.
Finally one morning he told, 'In dream Bhai Hira Singh Ji is calling from Sachkhand that come soon, we are waiting for you, so now my preparations are ready.' (Bhai Hira Singh Ji had passed away 7-8 months before). And on that very day he departed for Gurpuri. This incident is of **April 1927**.

**Bhai Ishar Singh Ji (1877-1980):**
According to his statement, he had acquired education from **Bhai Mahna Singh Ji** along with Bhai Hira Singh Ji. He told that, 'Bhai Hira Singh Ji was very fond of reading Gurbani and Gur-History and he used to remain ahead of all students. Since childhood his interest was towards reading religious texts.'
About Bhai Hira Singh Ji's father and grandfather also...
Started life from **Munshigiri** (clerkship) but his honesty and nobility...
His partners **Bhai Ladha Ram Ji Minocha** and his brother **Bhai Harnam Das**, **Wazir Chand** were also similar devoted Sikhs of Guru House, who used to take Guru's support before doing every work.
...lands were allotted in **(Jagadhri)**, where the first work **Bhai Ishar Singh Ji** did was to build a Gurdwara. Whenever anyone went to visit him, he [found him] serving in Gurdwara or reading Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji...
He passed away on **1.2.1980** at the age of about **103 years**...

## Notes
- The disputed land was won and named **"Panth Garh"**.
- Bhai Amar Singh's death in April 1927, guided by a vision of Bhai Hira Singh Ji.
- **Bhai Ishar Singh Ji**: Classmate of Bhai Hira Singh Ji, lived to 103 years.
