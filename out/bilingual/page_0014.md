## English (Meaning Translation)

Before starting the life story of Bhai Hira Singh Ji, it is very important to give the readers a little information about his birth land, so that... map comes before the eyes, where Bhai Sahib's birth took place, where he spent his childhood time and received education, and to raise the standard of educational and social life of which land he made many efforts.

Near the border of Jhang Shahpur in West Punjab (now Pakistan), a small town was settled, whose name 'Faruka' was based on a Muslim clan named 'Farooq' or caste. At the end of the nineteenth century, only four or five houses of 'Farookas' were left, and due to the ownership of most lands being with 'Kaliars', the chiefs of that area were 'Kaliars'.

In the time we are talking about, their chief was Allah Yar Kaliar. [He] was a God-fearing and noble man. Being the Zaildar of that whole area, he considered the population of [the area] as his subjects and shared in the joys and sorrows of the people.
His son was also a noble man, although he held good influence/awe.

With the arrival of canal and rail in the [area] and the colonization of Sargodha Bar, some [change came]. The railway station Silanwali was 9 miles away from Faruka. [To travel] from Faruka, there was no means other than camels or horses. In the [beginning of] the twentieth century, due to the labor of Bhai Hira Singh Ji, with the establishment of Khalsa High School, the road also became paved, and tumtums and tongas also started running.

[Whenever one mentions Faruka], the other name necessarily comes to the fore. Just as 'Bhai Hira Singh Ji Faruke Wale' or...
