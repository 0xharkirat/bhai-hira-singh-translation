## English (Meaning Translation)

...[People advised him against going], but the more they stopped Bhai Sahib, the more he insisted on going. Finally, he got ready to go with his Jatha.
In those days cars were not yet prevalent, so arrangements of horses and Tongas were made. Traveling overnight, they all reached the border of Yagistan area at Amrit Vela.

...Around Bhai Visakha Singh's house, gunman Pathans brought along were made to stand guard. Now **Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji** was installed (Prakash). This Holy Bir they had taken along from Hoti. Bhai Hira Singh Ji started Kirtan of **Asa Di Var**.
The sweetness [of his voice] and love-soaked explanation was casting a magical spell on the listeners.
He completed this Anand Karaj according to time, his distinctive wisdom, and such ability that the onlookers were overwhelmed.

**Amazing Scene:** When Bhai Hira Singh Ji was performing Kirtan, **Chaudhary Mahesha Mal** from the opposing faction of the village, either to see the spectacle of Gur-Maryada marriage or with the intention of creating disturbance, came and sat in the Sangat covering his face. The Amrit Kirtan of Bhai Sahib and the holy Gur-Maryada had such a magical effect on his mind that he got up and went out, and then in a short while came back to the Diwan bringing other companions of his faction along, and stood in the Sangat with folded hands and said, "If this is Gur-Maryada, then first of all we pledge to be followers of Gur-Maryada today."

According to the statement of **Baba Prem Singh Ji Hoti**, "This success had such an effect on Bhai Hira Singh Ji's mind that the glowing redness of his face turned into an unbearable spiritual radiance. At this time, in the passion of youth, he roared such thunderous **Jaikaras** (Bolay So Nihal), the echo of which made the local hills tremble. This was the effect of his captivating Kirtan, listening to which the whole village of staunch opponents became friends and loving devotees of Guru's house."
(Khalsa Samachar, 30 August 1951)
