## English (Meaning Translation)
...[he would] respect everyone and wish well for everyone.
Despite performing Kirtan for hours and being tired, he would never just leave the Diwan; instead, after descending from the stage, he would sit down nearby to listen with full concentration to the Kirtan, Katha, or lecture of other scholars and performers, and would attend the Diwan until the Bhog (conclusion). He believed that it was not appropriate to leave the Diwan without hearing the Hukam of Maharaj after Ardas.

**Respect for Teacher:** An estimate of Bhai Hira Singh Ji's respectful heart can be formed from the esteem he held for a Muslim teacher of his, as given in the 30.8.1951 issue of *Khalsa Samachar*.
It is an incident of 1912-13. Once Bhai Sahib went to Sargodha. Bhai Sudh Singh Ji was also there. Getting up from Kirtan at the Gurdwara, he took him along and buying a basket of fruits from the market, came to Government High School which was near the Gurdwara. There he went and found a Muslim teacher, Maulvi Munawar Deen. Bhai Hira Singh Ji bowed to meet him and presented the basket of fruits along with five rupees with folded hands. 
The Master (teacher) was somewhat surprised and said, "Sir, I do not understand, how have you come and who are you?"
Bhai Sahib said, "I am from Firuka, son of Bhai Bhag Singh, and you are my teacher. You used to teach me in the Government School at Firuka. When I heard that you are posted here now, I came to meet you along with my brother Bhai Sudh Singh. My name is Hira Singh and my father is..."
Hearing this, Maulvi Munawar Deen gave him love (blessed him) and said, "In this Kaljug (Dark Age), to come and meet specially... who knows these values nowadays? This is an angel. When did he study in first or second grade, and tracing that school time, to come and meet me saying 'he is my education-giver' (Vidya Data)? What a respecter of the teacher is this student?" And saying this, his eyes filled with tears.

Then that Master gentleman came to the Gurdwara at night to listen to Kirtan, and came again the next morning too. Listening again and again, he would say, "Subhan Allah! The community that has such divine preachers, angels, as national preachers, who sing the divine word of Guru Nanak and make others listen in ecstasy, swaying themselves—why wouldn't that community [spread] in the whole world?"
