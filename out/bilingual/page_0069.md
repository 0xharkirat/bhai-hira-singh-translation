## English (Meaning Translation)

...[Guru Granth Sahib's Sawari] was brought with great respect and devotion. Everyone showered flowers on Guru Baba...
Bhai Sahib's soul-touching sermons... praise of Gurbani was soaked [in love]...
It was a sample in itself. [He] filled the love of Guru's feet in many hearts and making them sacrifice their self, they came to Guru's shelter. Many abandoned poison (tobacco etc.) and all blessed themselves with the splash of Gurbani.
(Khalsa Samachar, 25 September and 9 October 1919)

According to the report of **Sardar Diwan Singh Headmaster Khalsa School Rangoon**, "Preaching in Upper Burma, on return Bhai Bhag Singh Hira Singh Ji reached **Rangoon** and for more than 20 days rain of Amrit and shower of Bani continued. Be it men or women... engaged in [Kirtan] Alap they seemed as if sold on the name of Waheguru...
Every being who had even a little influence of Sikhi, was drawn to the Kirtan due to Satguru's benevolence and did not like to miss it (put a Naga). The preaching had a very good effect and **64** Amrit-seeker men and women took Amrit..."
The solid service of Panth done by Bhai Sahib through Kirtan in [Burma?] is...
