## English (Meaning Translation)

'...today sweet sweet Bhai Hira Singh, Guru's unique Kirtaniya is coming to memory... was very loving with everyone and truly he was a trader of virtues... recipient of Guru's grace and a great player in sphere of No-Enmity (Nirvair)... Waheguru Ji had blessed him with knowledge... On his tongue Saraswati resided and in speech-composition he was like a master. He used to bind thousands of listeners like beads in a rosary... and wonder was that for four-four hours he would not let anyone move. There was sweet juice in his oratory, it was some spiritual earning...'

**Sant Sangat Singh Ji Kamalie Wale (1882-1950):**
Was a great spiritual [soul]... had dedicated himself for preaching. His speech had magical effect. When he used to do Katha of Shabad, explaining deep meanings and high ideals of Sikhism with proofs from Gurbani... listeners used to get extremely impressed.
Sri Sant Ji had deep love with Bhai Hira Singh Ji and... used to enjoy Satsang at religious Diwans of **Faruka**.
After Bhai Hira Singh Ji's departure to Gurpuri, on the occasion of Bhog he visited Faruka and in the end of his speech said:
"Bhai Hira Singh Ji was a **silkworm** who... while extracting silk (virtues) ended himself."

**Dr. Sahib (1876-1934):**
[Dr. Diwan Singh/Dr. Ratan Singh?] had an ideal life. ...was a top doctor. Beautiful well-built body, shining nature... [disease] used to go away just by [his touch/sight?]. People used to call him Angel of Health.
**Subedar Ratan Singh Ji**:
He was a military officer from **Dhamial (Rawalpindi)**...

- Tributes from **Sant Sangat Singh Ji Kamalie Wale**. He compared Bhai Hira Singh to a silkworm giving silk (virtues) to others while consuming itself.
