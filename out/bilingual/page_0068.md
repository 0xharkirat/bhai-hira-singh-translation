## English (Meaning Translation)

...He was a close acquaintance of the famous scholar and writer **Sardar Gobind Singh Mansukhani**. Along with this, he [mentioned] names of many other Gurmukhs whom Bhai Hira Singh Ji awakened and by administering Amrit made them Tyar-Bar-Tyar Singhs. Some names that Das (servant/author) remembers are - **Diwan Hira Singh**...

**Wave of Sikh Preaching in Burma:**
In **1919**, Bhai Hira Singh Ji got the opportunity to go to **Burma** with his Jatha, when he was invited by the Sangat of **Mandalay** to reach the Diwan. Here the Sikh population was second only to **Rangoon** and Sangats from other towns of Upper Burma also reached the Diwan in large numbers.
Here for the first time a **Nagar Kirtan** (Procession) was taken out with royal grandeur (Shah-i-Jalal). This was a new kind of program at that place, seeing which the residents there were highly impressed and attended the Diwan congregations in large numbers.
Great preaching of Gurmat took place.
On the last day of the Diwan, Bhai Sahib made a strong appeal for the establishment of a **Khalsa School** and by collecting thousands of rupees laid the foundation of the school.
...
[Invitation from Rangoon]
Accepting the invitation of Burma Sangats, [they went to] Rangoon...
[Nagar Kirtan was taken out with Guru Granth Sahib's Sawari...]

## Notes
- "Mandalay" and "Rangoon" are major cities in Burma (Myanmar).
