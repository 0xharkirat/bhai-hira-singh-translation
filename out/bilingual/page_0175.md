## English (Meaning Translation)
...[We] reached there [Chitraur] reading Shabad. A Gurmukh of that place and Bhai Sahib [had a connection?]...
Bhai Sahib had decorated the Diwan after reaching that land. He had also ordered me to recite a poem...
There, on a high mound of sand, in the shining sand of the jungle...
...and in the world of animals and birds, Bhai Sahib, while doing Kirtan, reached a state of ecstasy (Wajad). There, God seemed to be descending from the heavens and it felt as if people of all castes and religions sitting together...
...and feeling the Light of Waheguru through the sweet words of Gurbani, were giving practical proof of "Sabhe Sanjhiwal Sadayan" (All are called partners).
Among the listeners there, Muslims were in large numbers.
Today, when I bring that time before my eyes after about sixty years, when [He] actually used to walk and live among us and feel our sorrows and joys, wandering on this earth, I feel amazed.
When some historian of the future writes the history of Sikh Dharma and Gurmat preaching, he will be compelled to write the name of this Saint Preacher in golden letters because he spent his entire life showing the Sikh Path and, taking the Divine Light far and wide, made an effort to deliver the Gurmat Message to those thousands of people who, living in the darkness of ignorance, were becoming victims of superstitions and false entanglements.

**The Experienced Dhadi of Guru's House**
*S. Gurbakhsh Singh Ji, Bar At Law, Chandigarh,*
*'Ajit', 18 November 1979*
