## English (Meaning Translation)

...started. This time was the end of year **1897**.
In those days, **Giani Thakur Singh Ji** usually used to be with the Jatha of Bhai Bhag Singh Hira Singh Ji.
Where there is **Guru Ka Bagh** (Amritsar) now, there used to be an old-style [garden/park]. Here [they] had attached a [stage?] in front of which fountains used to flow. Daily evening, one satsang...

He had already studied... and Gur-History, and [whatever was lacking] he obtained from the company of **Sant Attar Singh Ji**. And the knowledge of Parmarth (spirituality) increased further in the Satsang of **Bhai Sahib Bhai Vir Singh Ji**. Life was molded in the service of Sri Darbar Sahib and the Master had blessed him with a melodious throat.
Due to these qualities, a strange charm used to bind in his Kirtan and the attendance of Sangat started increasing day by day. In a short time, the fame of his Kirtan [spread] in Amritsar...

**Rai Bahadur Bisakha Singh Ji** (1880-1959), famous contractor of Delhi, told in one of his articles (Sher-e-Punjab, 2 September 1951) - "In 1898 I heard his Kirtan for the first time when he was in youth stage at a wedding..."
The late **Bhai Sudh Singh Ji** told in one of his articles (Khalsa Samachar, 2-9-70) - "In 1901 when I was a student of Updeshak School Gujranwala, Bhai Bhag Singh Bhai Hira Singh Ji came there. The bloom of youth was on his Kirtan at that time... He had a nice full beautiful beard and his mood used to get emotional while performing Kirtan and Prayer... Sangat used to get lost... flying in that Mandal..."

...very sweet melodious... in his throat [was] Amrit...
'Gursikh Ashiq Sabad Re' (Gursikh is lover of the Word) - listening to [his] Kirtan, sacrificing body, mind and wealth...
In every Diwan/Samagam... magnetic pull in the Guru's Sangat...
