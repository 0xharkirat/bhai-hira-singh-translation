## English (Meaning Translation)

**Background**
We will start the mention of the father and grandfather of Bhai Hira Singh Ji from his Gurmukh beloved grandfather, **Bhai Jiwan Singh Ji**.

At the end of the Dharamsala street of Bhai Jeet Singh Ji, there was a small house where a Gurmukh hard-working Sikh used to live, whose auspicious name was Bhai Jiwan Singh. He was a famous gentleman in the area due to his Sikh character and Gurmat way of life.
To wake up in the last quarter of the night (early morning) and perform Nitnem and recitation of Sukhmani Sahib was his daily routine. Then he would attend Bhai Jeet Singh's Dharamsala and after taking the Hukam of Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji, he would engage in his business... Mata Ji was a great Gurmukh and server of Saints; she considered herself fortunate by doing this service.

The name of Bhai Jiwan Singh Ji's father was **Baba Channan Singh** and grandfather's name was **Bhai Devi Das**. Baba Channan Singh, along with his two elder sons, Prem Singh and Jiwan Singh, used to do the business of transporting goods on camels to the East (Purb Desh). The third son, Bhai Kalhu Ram, used to look after the house. From Khewra [salt mines] in West Punjab... Jaggery, Cloth and other goods used to go to the West.

In the year of the mutiny of **1857**, when Baba Jiwan Singh Ji had come to the East country with his elder brother Baba Prem Singh, they suffered a lot of loss at that time. Like many other traders of the area, these two brothers also returned home after a long time. Baba Prem Singh then left home and settled in Patiala, but Baba Jiwan Singh remained in his own home [Faruka] and started the work of farming... near Faruka...

Two children were born in the house of Baba Jiwan Singh. The first boy [was born] in 1844... whose name was kept **Bhag Singh**. After this a son...
Baba Ji completed his life journey and passed away in the year 1893 after living an age of about 73 years.
