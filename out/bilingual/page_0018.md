## English (Meaning Translation)

**Bhai Hira Singh Ji**, who was a strong supporter of Gur-Maryada, [made efforts]... and this way established the tradition of Anand Karaj in the area.
Where the preachers used to come and preach in the Diwans, there many other virtuous scholars also used to visit... Saint Attar Singh Ji, Saint Sangat Singh Ji... Bhai [Vir?] Singh Ji, Saint Gurbakhsh Singh Ji etc. put their feet on this land.

As a result of these religious Diwans, and with the efforts of Bhai Hira Singh Ji, the Kanya Pathshala (Girls' School) in 1906 and Khalsa High School in 1908 came into existence in Faruka.
The influence of the activities of Singh Sabha Faruka was bound to be on the nearby villages. In Silanwali, Sahiwal etc., Singh Sabhas were established and religious Diwans started to be held. In every small village of the area, wherever Sikhs [lived]...
These gentlemen used to attend the religious Diwans of Faruka and every year many people used to partake Amrit. Abandoning the Brahmanical rites prevalent in the whole area, the Sangat started following the objectives of Singh Sabha. At many places, like in a village of rich moneylenders named 'Pahlad', Gurmatas were passed that all rites of death etc. be done according to Gurmat; flowers (ashes) should not be picked on death nor should they be taken to Haridwar, rather collecting all the skeletal remains they should be flown in the nearby flowing Jhelum river; Shradhs-Navratras and other such rituals should be abandoned etc. Apart from this, the devotees here formed a Jatha for selfless service, which used to [serve] in the [Diwans] of the area, like celebrating Gurpurabs etc. and performing the service of Akhand Path without any offering, rather eating Prasada (food) from their own home and reaching on their own horses/transport.

**Participation in Akali Movement and Gurdwara Reform Movement:**
Many of the youths of Faruka and the area reached Sri Amritsar during the time of Gurdwara Reform Movement and dedicated their services to the Panth, and courted arrests in Guru Ka Bagh and Jaito Morchas.
